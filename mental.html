Mental Benefits of Coffee

The caffeine in coffee acts as a mild stimulant to the central nervous system:

  • General Alertness. The European Food Safety Authority (EFSA) concluded that a cause and effect relationship between improved alertness and attention and 75mg caffeine (the amount in a regular cup of coffee) had been established
  • Control Parkinson's disease symptoms.Caffeine can improve wakefulness in situations of reduced alertness or lack of sleep, for example; night-time driving, working at night, suffering from a cold5 and during the post-lunch dip
  • Sleeping disorders. In a sample of people under 40, a study found that caffeine or coffee may be effective in improving performance in those suffering from jet lag or shift work sleep disorder
  • Memory Performance. Some studies have shown that caffeine may enhance memory performance, particularly when tedious, repetitive tasks are involved. However, higher intake may decrease performance, possibly due to over-stimulation
